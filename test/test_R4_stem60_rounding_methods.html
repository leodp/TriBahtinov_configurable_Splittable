R4 Slits Near 60° Stem: Rounding Comparison

Scenario: BahF=150, Order=3, A_off=90, As=10. Each panel overlays rounded slit (cyan fill) with original slit outline (orange). Two slits shown per panel: the two R4 slits closest to the 60° stem boundary and R3.

Method Descriptions:
M1 (Current): Analytical arc-line tangency; works for many cases but fails for mixed edge types (stem+curve).
M2 (Guarded): M1 + validation checks; best current method but still skips sharp curved-surface vertices due to collinearity filtering.
M3 (Simple Dual-Tangency): Iterative search for maximum radius by placing circle tangent to straight edge, moving inward until it touches curved surface. Bypasses convexity checks.
M4 (Refined Dual-Tangency): M3 with clamped radius for stability; uses fallback bisector for non-mixed edges.
M5 (Aggressive Dual-Tangency): M3 with higher radius ceiling for maximum rounding; more aggressive than M4.
M6 (Bisector-Only): Uses angle bisector for all corners; baseline for comparison.
